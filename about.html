About Conduit

Building stronger communities through accessible, centralized information about local resources and services.

Our Purpose

Conduit was created to address a critical need in our communities: accessible, centralized information about local resources and services. Many residents struggle to find help when they need it most, simply because they don't know where to look.

Our mission is to bridge this information gap by providing a comprehensive, easy-to-use platform where anyone can discover community resources including food assistance, healthcare, education, employment services, housing support, and legal aid.

Research & Development Process

The development of Conduit was guided by extensive research into community needs and best practices for resource hubs:

  • Community Surveys: We analyzed feedback from community members about challenges in finding local resources
  • Nonprofit Partnerships: Collaborated with local organizations to understand the most critical services needed
  • User Experience Research: Studied successful civic platforms to identify key features and design patterns
  • Accessibility Studies: Reviewed WCAG guidelines and inclusive design principles to ensure our platform serves all users
  • Geographic Analysis: Mapped resource distribution to identify service gaps in the community

Web Technologies Used

Conduit is built using modern, accessible web technologies to ensure reliability, performance, and ease of use:

HTML5
CSS3
JavaScript (ES6+)
Leaflet.js
OpenStreetMap
LocalStorage API

Why Vanilla JavaScript? We chose to build without frameworks to ensure maximum performance, minimal load times, and a lightweight footprint. This approach makes the platform accessible even on slower internet connections.

Key Features

  • Searchable Directory: Live search and filtering to quickly find relevant resources
  • Interactive Map: Visual representation of resource locations using OpenStreetMap integration
  • Category Filtering: Browse resources by type including food, healthcare, education, and more
  • Community Submissions: Allow residents to contribute new resources through a validated form
  • Responsive Design: Optimized for all devices from mobile phones to desktop computers
  • Client-Side Storage: Submissions stored locally for immediate confirmation and future review

Accessibility Features

We believe everyone deserves equal access to community information. Our platform includes:

  • Semantic HTML: Proper heading hierarchy and ARIA labels for screen readers
  • Keyboard Navigation: Full functionality without requiring a mouse
  • Color Contrast: WCAG AA compliant color combinations for readability
  • Responsive Typography: Text scales appropriately across all device sizes
  • Focus Indicators: Clear visual feedback for interactive elements
  • Alt Text: Descriptive text for all images and icons
  • Mobile-First Design: Optimized for the devices most commonly used by community members

Future Enhancements

As Conduit grows, we plan to add:

  • Multilingual support for non-English speakers
  • User accounts for saving favorite resources
  • Real-time hours and availability updates
  • Resource reviews and ratings from community members
  • Integration with local transportation for directions
  • SMS and email notifications for new resources

TSA Webmaster Competition

This project was developed for the Technology Student Association (TSA) Webmaster competition, which challenges students to create websites that address real-world community needs. Conduit represents our commitment to using technology as a force for social good.
